Day 1: Afternoon Arrival and City Introduction map

Day 1: Afternoon Arrival and City Introduction

Afternoon

  • 2:00 PM – Arrival and Check-In: Land at KCH, transfer to your hotel (15–20 minutes by Grab, ~RM 15). Settle in and freshen up.
  • 3:30 PM – Kuching Waterfront: Stroll along the Sarawak River esplanade. See the Astana, Darul Hana Bridge, and cat statues. Grab coffee or juice at The White Barouk café.

Evening

  • 5:30 PM – Sunset River Cruise: 1-hour cruise (RM 60–80). See Fort Margherita, skyline views. Includes light snacks.
  • 7:00 PM – Dinner at Topspot Food Court: Jalan Padungan. Enjoy seafood and Sarawak dishes. Budget: RM 20–40 per person.
  • 8:30 PM – Carpenter Street: Explore historic area, Tua Pek Kong Temple, Main Bazaar for souvenirs. Return to hotel by 9:30 PM.

Tips: Bring cash for vendors. Wear light, comfortable clothing (28–30°C).

Day 2: Nature and Wildlife

Morning

  • 7:30 AM – Breakfast at Choon Hui Cafe: Try Sarawak laksa with popiah or kaya toast. Budget: RM 10–15.
  • 8:30 AM – Semenggoh Wildlife Centre: 30-minute drive. Orangutan feeding at 9 AM (RM 10). Arrive by 7:45 AM. Depart by 10:30 AM.

Lunch

  • 11:30 AM – Lepau Restaurant: Try ayam pansuh, stir-fried pako, or vegetarian dishes. Budget: RM 20–40.

Afternoon

  • 1:00 PM – Bako National Park: 35-minute drive + 25-minute boat. Hike Paku trail (1–2 hrs), spot proboscis monkeys. Depart by 4 PM. Entry RM 20 + Boat RM 40.

Evening

  • 6:30 PM – Dinner at Pinggai Cafe: Try kampua mee or kolo mee. Budget: RM 10–20.
  • 8:00 PM – Waterfront Relax: Gula apong ice cream near Jalan Gambier. Enjoy river breeze.

Tips: Book Bako boats via Kampung Bako. Semenggoh morning sessions are quieter.

Day 3: Cultural Immersion and Beach

Day 3: Cultural Immersion and Beach

Morning

  • 8:30 AM – Breakfast: Hotel or try kolo mee at Sin Lian Shin (Jalan Padungan).
  • 9:30 AM – Sarawak Cultural Village: 45-minute drive. Traditional homes, 11:30 AM dance show, blowpipe shooting. Entry RM 60.

Lunch

  • 1:00 PM – Damai Central Food Court: Kueh chap or grilled fish. Budget: RM 15–25.

Afternoon

  • 2:00 PM – Damai Beach: Relax, kayak (~RM 20/hour), stroll. No swimming due to currents.
  • 4:00 PM – Cat Museum: 20-minute drive. Visit 4,000+ cat-themed exhibits. RM 3 entry, 45 minutes.

Evening

  • 6:30 PM – Siniawan Night Market (Fri–Sun only): 30-minute drive. Try pork satay, fried quail. Budget: RM 10–20. If not open, dine at The White Barouk (RM 30–50).
  • 9:00 PM – Return to Kuching: Rest at hotel.

Tips: Bring a hat and water. Siniawan is best on weekends.

Day 4: Museum and Departure

Day 4: Museums and Departure

Morning

  • 8:30 AM – Breakfast at Open Air Market: Try kolo mee or roti canai. Budget: RM 8–15.
  • 9:30 AM – Borneo Cultures Museum: Exhibits on culture and biodiversity. RM 20–25. Allow 1.5–2 hours.
  • 11:30 AM – Fort Margherita: 5-minute boat ride (RM 2). 19th-century fort with museum.

Lunch

  • 12:30 PM – The Granary Kitchen + Bar: Sarawak laksa pizza, smoked duck salad. Budget: RM 20–40.

Afternoon

  • 2:00 PM – Last-Minute Shopping: Main Bazaar for crafts, Plaza Merdeka for modern stores. Grab Sarawak pepper or kek lapis.
  • 3:30 PM – Depart for Airport: Grab to KCH (~RM 15). Arrive by 4:00 PM for check-in.

Tips: Museums close by 5 PM. Adjust timing based on flight.

General Notes

  • Weather: May is warm (28–32°C) with possible showers. Bring umbrella and light clothing.
  • Transport: Grab rides RM 5–15 in city, RM 30–50 to parks. Car rental ~RM 100/day.
  • Budget: Estimate RM 150–250/day per person (excluding accommodation).
  • Connectivity: Wi-Fi in hotels/cafés. Local SIMs (e.g., Celcom) ~RM 20 for 10GB.
